Pathway (summary)

Partnering with academic & medical researchers

Why

I believe everyone deserves the same chance at a good health outcome.

What

I partnered with Cincinnati Childrens Hospital and the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation to transform the CF community into a learning health system where everyone impacted by the systems of care (patients, parents, clinicians, researchers, etc) is involved in the design of and systematic testing of ideas and resources that have the potential to improve health and care. Data is collected for learning and teams collaborate nationally to implement the ideas that have proven to improve outcomes faster.

How

Through engagement with peers online, I recognized the unintended variation in care and outcomes across the CF community despite having a strong advocacy organization, accredited care center network and patient registry.

Lessons learned

  1. Academics do not move fast at anything they do.
  2. It’s nearly impossible to get funding to support your time and work before you have shown the value you bring to an organization - starting as a volunteer with a passion is more productive in the long run, the money will follow.

Vision

Learning health systems exist across a variety of conditions and even social service areas (home visiting, education, for example). There is much efficiency to be found in cross-network learning, but not a good mechanism for doing so. That's what I want to do next.
