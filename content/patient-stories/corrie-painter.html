Pathway (summary)

Connecting cancer patients and researchers in order to co-design studies that generate large scale clinically annotated genomics data for deposition in the public domain.

Why

After being diagnosed with angiosarcoma in 2010, I realized we needed more and better data that everyone had access to.

What

Joined the Broad Institute to help launch Count Me In, a patient-partnered movement that engages patients in the design and implementation of cancer genomics studies.

How

Building off my background as a scientist and patient/advocate, I asked for LOTs of help from patient experts, built out communication streams between them and the researchers at the Broad, and launched our first project, the Metastatic Breast Cancer Project (MBCproject.org).

Lessons learned

  • Part of the job description said 'risk taker'. I gave up a traditional career as an academic research scientist to risk it all by charting a path that didn't exist. It was unnerving, but totally worth it.
  • I cry at least once a day due to the constant unwavering death of people I've come to know and love through this work. There's no avoiding a broken heart when working with terminally ill patients.

Vision

Build these in all cancers, then all diseases, until we have an infrastructure in place that enables maximum acceleration of research.
