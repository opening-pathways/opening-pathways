Pathway (summary)

Creating a non-profit organization to speed research and therapeutic development for nonsense mutations of Cystic Fibrosis (CF).

Why

There are game-changing breakthroughs for roughly 90% of the CF population, but I belong to the outlying 10% for whom there is still nothing. I believe we deserve better.

What

Created a non-profit organization to fund, accelerate, and drive more research and drug development for nonsense mutations of Cystic Fibrosis.

How

I saw a lack of focus, leadership and coordination for nonsense mutations of CF. Given the progressive nature of CF, we realized that people with nonsense mutations of CF didn't have time to wait idly. We need progress and we needed fast, and if nobody else was going to do it with the urgency and focus that we believe the space needed, then it was up to us.

Lessons learned

  • You don’t know what you don’t know. Mentorship is helpful.

  • You may need to plan to talk or prepare the ground for a conversation about a new idea 3-4 times before someone else can really dive into the new idea with you, because it’s so different. Also, you may want to emotionally prepare for the response you get from the first time you share an idea.

  • You may need to translate the language you use to express your ideas into scientific or business language to be more easily understood by those you are working with. Also, it can be helpful to share examples of other people or organizations who are doing similar things in a different space to help people understand what you are proposing.

Vision

My dream is to cure CF and move on to another rare disease in another area, applying our models for research that we’ve learned through CF.
