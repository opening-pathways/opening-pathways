Pathway (summary)

Creating an open source movement and encouraging open source development in more areas of healthcare.

Why

Frustration with current “gold standard” diabetes devices that weren't meeting my needs.

What

Open sourced (publicly shared) code & instructions for others to build their own APS, built and support a community to reach others who wanted to do the same

How

One step at a time - building my own solution; sharing it; helping the next person; building the tools and documentation that would scale and allow us to support more people, without needing funding or an organization to do so

Lessons learned

  • This was and is an unpaid labor of love. It takes a lot of time, and I was in a position of privilege to be able to spend the time to do so.

  • The community building & support were & are as important as the technology itself.

Vision

There are now several thousand individuals using DIYAPS, and versions are being tested in research studies including clinical trials and potentially being commercialized. What comes next is also helping other people in other healthcare communities figuring out the right pathway for sharing their ideas & solutions
